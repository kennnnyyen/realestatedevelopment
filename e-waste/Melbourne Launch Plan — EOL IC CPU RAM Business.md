### OPTION A — Extend Your Existing NSW Business into Victoria

**The short answer: yes, you can operate Melbourne under your existing NSW entity from day one.**

An Australian ABN is national — it is not state-restricted. Your NSW-registered business can legally trade, sign contracts, issue invoices, and collect e-waste in Victoria without registering a new entity, new ABN, or new company. Melbourne is simply a new operating location of your existing business.

**What this looks like operationally:**
- You collect EOL hardware from Melbourne SMBs under your existing business name
- You store and grade it at the Melbourne warehouse
- You ship it to your existing overseas buyers via your existing export logistics chain
- Revenue flows directly into your existing accounts and BAS

Here is what that means for setup:

| Area | What You Need To Do | What You Don't Need To Do |
|------|--------------------|-----------------------------|
| **ABN** | Nothing — your existing ABN covers all Australian states | Register a new ABN or company |
| **GST / BAS** | Nothing — Melbourne revenue rolls into your existing GST registration and BAS lodgements | Separate GST registration for VIC |
| **Business name** | Nothing — your existing registered business name is valid nationally | Re-register the name in VIC |
| **Contracts & invoices** | Use your existing templates; update the address to show your Melbourne warehouse | Rewrite service agreements |
| **Tax** | Melbourne income is reported under your existing tax file number and entity | Separate VIC tax filing |
| **Workers compensation** | **One action:** notify your existing insurer to add VIC as an operating state — typically a free policy endorsement | Separate WorkCover policy |
| **Public liability insurance** | **One action:** confirm your existing policy covers operations in Victoria (most national policies already do) | New insurance policy |
| **Payroll tax** | VIC threshold is A$900K/year in wages — well below that at launch. No action needed initially | Register for VIC payroll tax at launch |

**Victorian-specific operational items (still needed regardless of structure):**

| Victorian-Specific Item | Action Required |
|------------------------|-----------------|
| **EPA Victoria — waste storage** | Call EPA Vic Business Hotline **1300 372 842** to confirm your initial volumes sit below the storage permit threshold. At SMB-scale launch, almost certainly no permit required. |
| **Victorian Waste Carrier** | If running your own pickup vehicle in VIC, it must be registered as a waste transporter in Victoria. If using a courier/3PL for early pickups, this is not required. |
| **Waste Transfer Certificate (VIC format)** | Victoria has its own WTC format. Update your Sydney template to the VIC version — published by EPA Victoria. Approximately 30 minutes of work. |

> **Bottom line for Option A:** You can start trading in Melbourne next week. The only pre-launch actions are: (1) call your insurer to confirm VIC coverage, (2) call EPA Victoria about storage thresholds, (3) update your WTC template. ABN, GST, contracts, and invoicing are already done.

**Best for:** Moving fast, keeping admin simple, one set of books, treating Melbourne as an extension of what you already do.

### OPTION B — Set Up a Separate Melbourne Entity as a Middleman

This option involves registering a **new, separate Australian company** that operates purely as a **broker/middleman** between Melbourne supply sources and your NSW business (which remains the exporter). The Melbourne entity buys EOL hardware locally, then on-sells it to your NSW company, which handles the overseas export.

**Why you might choose this:**
- You want Melbourne to be a standalone business — separate P&L, separate risk profile, potentially a different partner or investor involved
- You want to isolate Victorian EPA/compliance obligations from your NSW entity
- You want to build a Melbourne brand independently (e.g. different trading name targeting local SMBs)
- Tax or structuring advice suggests it (speak to your accountant — there may be asset protection or tax benefits depending on your structure)

**How the middleman model works:**

```
Melbourne SMBs / ITAD aggregators
        ↓  (sell EOL hardware)
  NEW Melbourne Pty Ltd  ← buys, grades, holds stock
        ↓  (sells graded lots at transfer price)
  Your NSW Business  ← exports to overseas buyers
        ↓
  Overseas buyers (existing relationships)
```

The Melbourne entity earns its margin on the buy-sell spread between what it pays SMBs and what it charges your NSW business. Your NSW entity earns its margin on the export spread. Profits are reported separately in each entity.

**What you need to set up Option B:**

| Step | Detail | Timeframe | Cost |
|------|--------|-----------|------|
| **Register new Pty Ltd** | Via ASIC — register a new proprietary limited company. Can be done online in 1–2 business days. | Day 1–2 | A$576 ASIC fee |
| **Apply for new ABN** | Via ABR (abr.gov.au) — takes minutes online, issued within 1–5 business days | Day 1–2 | Free |
| **Register for GST** | If expecting >A$75K/year revenue from Melbourne entity (very likely) — register for GST at the same time as ABN | Day 1–2 | Free |
| **Open a business bank account** | Separate account for Melbourne entity. Most banks can open a business account within 1–3 business days online. | Week 1 | Free |
| **Register business name (if different)** | Via ASIC — if you want a different trading name for Melbourne. 1 year = A$42, 3 years = A$98 | Week 1 | A$42–98 |
| **Intercompany agreement** | A simple document that sets out the transfer pricing arrangement between Melbourne Pty Ltd and your NSW entity — how lots are priced, payment terms, liability. Get your accountant or a commercial lawyer to draft this. | Week 1–2 | A$500–1,500 legal |
| **Public liability insurance** | New policy for the Melbourne entity. ~A$800–2,000/year. | Week 1 | A$800–2,000 |
| **WorkCover VIC** | If hiring Melbourne staff under the new entity. | Week 1 | Via insurer |
| **EPA Victoria** | Same as Option A — call to confirm storage permit thresholds | Week 1 | Free |
| **VIC Waste Transfer Certificate** | Same as Option A — Melbourne entity issues VIC-format WTCs to its customers | Day 1–2 | Free |
| **VIC Waste Carrier** | Same as Option A — register pickup vehicle if operating own transport | Week 1–2 | Minimal |

**Total estimated setup time:** 1–2 weeks (most steps can run in parallel)
**Total estimated setup cost:** A$2,000–4,500 (ASIC fee + insurance + legal for intercompany agreement)

> **Important — Transfer Pricing:** Because the two entities are related parties, the ATO requires that the price at which Melbourne sells stock to your NSW business reflects a genuine commercial rate (i.e. what an independent party would pay). This is called transfer pricing. It doesn't need to be complex at this scale, but your accountant should document the methodology to avoid scrutiny. Keep it simple: cost + fixed margin %.

**Best for:** Wanting a separate Melbourne identity, isolating risk between states, bringing in a Melbourne-based partner, or building the Melbourne business to potentially sell or spin off later.

### 1.3 Key Documents to Prepare (Templates)

These documents are what convert SMB clients from "interested" to "signed up":

1. **Waste Transfer Certificate / Collection Receipt** — Given to every client at point of pickup. Proves their e-waste was collected by a licensed operator and diverted from landfill. This is what they need for their own compliance under the Victorian ban.
2. **Service Agreement (1-page)** — Covers: what you collect, turnaround time, data destruction commitment, liability. Keep it simple for SMBs.
3. **Data Destruction Statement** — Even for CPUs/RAM that aren't going to be repurposed, clients need a statement that data-bearing devices were handled appropriately (especially if servers have CPUs still in situ on boards with storage nearby).
4. **ESG Impact Statement (optional but powerful)** — Monthly summary: kg of material collected, estimated CO2 offset, components recovered vs. sent to scrap. SMBs increasingly need this for their own ESG reporting.

### 3.2 Call Scripts for Each Partner Type

**For ITAD operators (A Tech, ReTech, Excess Technology, RenovaTech):**

These operators process hardware then send it to bulk scrap. For every server they process, $50–$300+ in component value is being lost at $2/kg scrap rates.

> **Script:**
> "Hi — my name is [Name], I run an EOL hardware component recovery business and we've just opened a facility in Melbourne. We're looking to set up a standing buy arrangement with ITAD operators — we buy extracted CPUs, RAM, and server boards from your inbound lots at trade rates. You get incremental revenue on material that's currently going to scrap, and we handle the component side. Is that worth a quick conversation?"

---

**For surplus brokers (CRB Networks, Reused IT Storages):**

They buy and resell at the device level. When a device can't be resold intact, it goes to scrap. You want the component lots they can't move.

> **Script:**
> "Hi — we're a wholesale buyer of EOL CPUs, RAM, and server components, and we've just opened in Melbourne. For server boards or component lots you can't resell as complete devices — we'd be interested in a bulk buy arrangement at agreed trade rates. Do you have lots like that building up at the moment?"

---

**For component specialists (Support Components, Techbuyer):**

These can be two-way relationships — buyer and supplier depending on what's flowing.

> **Script:**
> "Hi — we're an EOL hardware collector based in Melbourne. We source bulk CPU, RAM, and IC inventory from ITAD operators and SMBs. Looking to establish a trade account — sometimes we'll be selling to you, sometimes buying specific EOL IC types you're sourcing. Can we explore a trade account in both directions?"

### 3.3 First Meeting — What to Cover

Keep it to 30 minutes. Agenda:
1. What you collect and how you process it (show photos from your Sydney operation)
2. What you're buying and at what grade criteria
3. Logistics — you pick up, or they deliver to your warehouse
4. Payment terms — EFT within agreed days of grading
5. Paperwork — compliance cert, lot receipt you provide

**Goal:** Leave with an agreed trial lot. One pickup or one lot to grade and buy. A standing arrangement follows once trust is established.
